New York Times Co. v. Sullivan (1964)

A Landmark Case for the U.S. First Amendment

This landmark Supreme Court decision established the "actual malice" standard, which requires public officials to prove that defamatory statements were made with knowledge of their falsity or with reckless disregard for the truth. This ruling significantly strengthened First Amendment protections for freedom of the press.

Original Advertisement

Replica of The New York Times full-page advertisement from March 29, 1960. Click on highlighted text to see fact-checking information.

HEED THEIR RISING VOICES

"In Montgomery, Alabama, after students sang 'My Country, 'Tis of Thee' on the State Capitol steps, their leaders were expelled from school, and truckloads of police armed with shotguns and tear-gas ringed the Alabama State College Campus. When the entire student body protested to state authorities by refusing to re-register, their dining hall was padlocked in an attempt to starve them into submission."

"Again and again the Southern violators have answered Dr. King's peaceful protests with intimidation and violence. They have bombed his home almost killing his wife and child. They have assaulted his person. They have arrested him seven times—for 'speeding,' 'loitering' and similar 'offenses.' And now they have charged him with 'perjury'—a felony under which they could imprison him for ten years...."

The New York Times, March 29, 1960, Page 25

Fact Check: 'My Country, 'Tis of Thee'

The students sang the national anthem "The Star-Spangled Banner", not "My Country, 'Tis of Thee".

事实核查：学生当时唱的是美国国歌《星条旗永不落》，而非《我的祖国，属于你》.

Fact Check: 'ringed the Alabama State College Campus'

Police were deployed near campus on several occasions but never "ringed" the campus at any time.

事实核查：警方曾数次在校园附近大规模部署，但从未在任何时间形成"包围"态势.

Fact Check: 'their dining hall was padlocked'

The campus dining hall was never padlocked. All students had access.

事实核查：校园食堂从未被锁上。任何学生均可进入.

Fact Check: 'seven times'

At the time of the advertisement, Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. had actually been arrested four times, not seven.

事实核查：在广告刊登时，马丁·路德·金博士实际被捕次数为四次，而非七次.

Issue 1: Whether State Libel Law Violates Freedom of Speech and Press

Sullivan & Alabama Side
  • State libel law is valid (presumes falsity/malice, defendants prove truth)
  • 1st Amendment doesn't protect libel; 14th Amendment ignores private libel

1. 州诽谤法合法（推定虚假 / 恶意，被告自证真实）
2. 第一修正案不护诽谤，第十四修正案不管私人诽谤

NYT & Supreme Court Side
  • State law violates 1st/14th Amendments (unfair burden, chills debate)
  • Civil rights ad deserves protection despite minor factual errors

1. 州法违宪（举证不公，压制辩论）
2. 民权广告虽有小错，仍应受宪法保护

Issue 2: Boundary Between Public Official Reputation and Public Oversight Rights

Sullivan & Alabama Side
  • Criticizing police = criticizing Sullivan's work
  • False claims about his agency harm his reputation (actionable)

1. 批评警方即批评沙利文履职
2. 涉其机构的不实言论可索赔

NYT & Supreme Court Side
  • Officials must accept work-related criticism (democratic oversight)
  • Official reputation can't override public debate rights

1. 公职人员须接受履职批评（民主监督）
2. 公职名誉不能凌驾于公众辩论权

Issue 3: Whether Requiring Proof of "Actual Malice" is Reasonable

Sullivan & Alabama Side
  • Presuming malice is a long-standing state rule (ensures accountability)
  • "Actual malice" standard lets media avoid liability for false claims

1. 推定恶意是州法惯例（保障追责）
2. "实际恶意" 标准会让媒体不实言论脱责

NYT & Supreme Court Side
  • Standard prevents self-censorship (critics don't need absolute truth)
  • Balances oversight/reputation (only punishes intentional falsehoods)

1. 该标准避免自我审查（无需证明绝对真实）
2. 平衡监督权与名誉权（仅罚故意不实言论）

The Ruling

Overturned Alabama Decision

The Supreme Court overturned Alabama courts' decisions, ruling that Alabama's libel rules failed to protect speech and press freedoms and were unconstitutional.

联邦最高法院推翻了亚拉巴马州各级法院的判决，认定该州的诽谤规则未保障言论与新闻自由，不符合宪法规定。

"Actual Malice" Rule

It created the "actual malice" rule: a public official can only get damages for defamatory lies about their work if they prove the speaker had actual malice.

确立 "实际恶意" 规则：公职人员只有举证证明表述发布方存在 "实际恶意"，才能就与其履职相关的诽谤性虚假表述获赔。

Burden of Proof Shifted

The burden of proof shifted to the official (plaintiff), not the media (defendant).

举证责任转移至公职人员原告一方，而非由媒体被告承担。

Significance

Landmark First Amendment Protection

It was a landmark case that strengthened First Amendment protections for media and citizens criticizing public officials.

这是一起里程碑案件，强化了宪法第一修正案对媒体和公民批评公职人员的权利保护

Extended Protections to States

It made clear that the Fourteenth Amendment extends First Amendment speech protections to state courts, limiting states' power to issue huge libel damages.

明确了宪法第十四修正案将第一修正案的言论自由保障延伸至州法院，限制了各州施加高额诽谤赔偿金的权力。

"Actual Malice" Standard Applied Broadly

The "actual malice" rule became a key part of U.S. defamation law and later applied to public figures beyond elected officials.

"实际恶意" 规则成为美国诽谤法核心准则，后续还拓展适用于公职人员之外的公众人物。

5 Typical Sentence Patterns

We hold that...

本院认为……

"We hold that a public official may not recover damages for a defamatory falsehood relating to his official conduct unless he proves that the statement was made with 'actual malice'."

学习要点：是法院发布有约束力裁判规则或最终结论的核心司法用语，具有权威性，常用于判决书 "裁判要点" 部分，后接具体法律规则或结论。

[Rule] is inconsistent with [constitutional provision]

[某规则] 与 [宪法条款] 相抵触

"Such a presumption [of malice under Alabama law] is inconsistent with the federal rule protected by the First and Fourteenth Amendments."

学习要点：用于宣告某法律或规则违宪的核心句型，需明确指出规则与宪法条款的冲突点，体现宪法对州立法和司法权的约束，常见于宪法性案件的法律分析部分。

[Right] is essential to [democratic value]

[某权利] 对 [民主价值] 至关重要

"Freedom of speech on public issues is essential to the maintenance of a responsive and accountable government."

学习要点：用于将宪法权利与民主核心原则建立关联的句型，强调权利的基础性价值，可用于论证权利保护的必要性，多见于裁判文书的说理部分。

The burden of proof rests with [party] to prove [fact]

[某方] 需承担证明 [某事实] 的举证责任

"The burden of proof rests with the public official to prove that the defendant acted with actual malice in publishing the defamatory statement."

学习要点：用于明确诉讼中举证责任分配的标准句型，需清晰区分举证主体和待证事实，是厘清诉讼双方责任的关键表达，常见于证据认定相关的裁判内容。

[Conduct] is protected by the First Amendment because [reason]

[某行为] 因 [某理由] 受第一修正案保护

"The advertisement is protected by the First Amendment because it addresses a matter of public concern and was published without actual malice."

学习要点：用于衔接具体行为与第一修正案保护的框架句型，需说明行为符合宪法保护的核心理由，是言论自由类案件法律适用的核心表达。

Question 1: Which of the following is the definition of "actual malice" under the ruling of New York Times Co. v. Sullivan?
A. Intent to harm a public official's personal reputation
B. Knowledge that a statement is false or reckless disregard for its truth
C. Failure to verify the accuracy of a statement before publication
D. Refusal to retract a statement after receiving a correction demand
Correct Answer: B

Explanation: The Supreme Court's ruling in New York Times Co. v. Sullivan defines "actual malice" as the state of mind where the speaker knows a statement is false or acts with reckless disregard for whether it is true or false.

Question 2: What type of damages does Alabama law require a retraction demand for before a public official can recover?
A. Compensatory damages
B. Nominal damages
C. Punitive damages
D. Statutory damages
Correct Answer: C

Explanation: Under Alabama law (Alabama Code, Tit. 7, §914), a public official must first issue a written retraction demand to claim punitive damages in a libel action related to their official conduct.

Question 3: Which constitutional amendment applies the First Amendment's speech protections to state courts?
A. Fifth Amendment
B. Fourteenth Amendment
C. Tenth Amendment
D. Twenty-First Amendment
Correct Answer: B

Explanation: The Fourteenth Amendment applies the First Amendment's speech and press freedoms to state courts, restricting states from enforcing laws that infringe on these constitutional rights.

Question 4: A statement that is inherently defamatory and actionable without proof of actual harm is called:
A. Libel per se
B. Seditious libel
C. Defamatory falsehood
D. Malicious publication
Correct Answer: A

Explanation: Libel per se refers to statements that are inherently defamatory and actionable without the need to prove actual pecuniary harm, such as attacks on an official's integrity or professional reputation.

Question 5: For a public official to win a libel suit against media, who bears the burden of proving "actual malice"?
A. The media defendant
B. The trial court jury
C. The public official plaintiff
D. The state attorney general
Correct Answer: C

Explanation: The ruling shifts the burden of proof to the public official (plaintiff), who must demonstrate that the media defendant acted with "actual malice" to win a libel claim.
